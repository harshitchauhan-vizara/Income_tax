SECTION 1: OVERVIEW
---
- The Income Tax Act, 2025 replaces the Income Tax Act, 1961. Effective April 1, 2026.
- 536 sections (reduced from 800+ in the 1961 Act) — simpler, cleaner drafting.
- Introduces a single "tax year" replacing the dual "previous year / assessment year" terminology.
- No change in tax policy, rates, or deduction limits — purely structural simplification.
- Tax Year 2026-27 = April 1, 2026 to March 31, 2027.
- First ITR under the 2025 Act due July 31, 2027.

---
SECTION 2: TAX SLABS AND RATES (FY 2026-27)
---

NEW TAX REGIME (DEFAULT from AY 2024-25, continued under 2025 Act):
  Up to ₹3,00,000        → Nil
  ₹3,00,001 – ₹7,00,000  → 5%
  ₹7,00,001 – ₹10,00,000 → 10%
  ₹10,00,001 – ₹12,00,000→ 15%
  ₹12,00,001 – ₹15,00,000→ 20%
  Above ₹15,00,000       → 30%
  Section 87A rebate makes income up to ₹7,00,000 effectively tax-free (rebate = ₹25,000).

OLD TAX REGIME (must be explicitly chosen):
  Individuals below 60 years:
    Up to ₹2,50,000       → Nil
    ₹2,50,001 – ₹5,00,000 → 5%
    ₹5,00,001 – ₹10,00,000→ 20%
    Above ₹10,00,000      → 30%
  Senior Citizens (60–79 years): Basic exemption ₹3,00,000
  Super Senior Citizens (80+ years): Basic exemption ₹5,00,000
  87A rebate available up to ₹5,00,000 income in old regime.

SURCHARGE ON INCOME TAX:
  ₹50 lakh – ₹1 crore    → 10%
  ₹1 crore – ₹2 crore    → 15%
  ₹2 crore – ₹5 crore    → 25%
  Above ₹5 crore          → 37% (old regime) / 25% (new regime — capped)
  LTCG / STCG on equity shares: surcharge capped at 15%.

HEALTH AND EDUCATION CESS: 4% on (tax + surcharge) — all taxpayers, all regimes.

CORPORATE TAX:
  Domestic companies (concessional, 115BAA): 22% + 10% surcharge + 4% cess ≈ 25.17%
  New manufacturing companies (115BAB): 15% + 10% surcharge + 4% cess ≈ 17.01%
  Default domestic companies: 30% + surcharge (7% for ₹1–10Cr; 12% above ₹10Cr) + 4% cess
  Foreign companies: 40% + surcharge + 4% cess

PARTNERSHIP FIRMS / LLPs: 30% flat + surcharge + 4% cess. No basic exemption.

SECTION 3: OLD vs NEW TAX REGIME
---
New regime = DEFAULT. Lower rates. Most deductions NOT available.
Old regime = OPTIONAL (must declare). Higher rates. ALL deductions available.

DEDUCTIONS AVAILABLE IN NEW REGIME (only these):
  - Standard deduction: ₹75,000 (salaried / pensioners)
  - Employer NPS contribution (80CCD(2)): 14% of salary (govt employees); 10% (others)
  - Family pension deduction: lower of one-third of pension or ₹25,000
  - Agniveer Corpus Fund contributions
  - Transport allowance for specially-abled employees
  - Conveyance/daily allowance for official duty

NOT available in new regime: 80C, 80D, HRA, LTA, home loan interest (Section 24b).

SWITCHING RULES:
  Salaried (no business income): Can switch every year.
  Business/professional income: Can switch from old to new ONCE. If switched back to old, cannot return to new.

WHICH REGIME TO CHOOSE:
  General rule: If total deductions > ~₹3.75 lakh → old regime likely saves more.
  If deductions are minimal → new regime is better.
  Always compute both before deciding. Use calculator at https://www.incometax.gov.in.

SECTION 4: HEADS OF INCOME
---
Five heads:
  1. Salaries — wages, pension, gratuity, perquisites, allowances, ESOPs
  2. Income from House Property — rental income from owned properties
  3. Profits and Gains from Business or Profession
  4. Capital Gains — profit from sale of capital assets
  5. Income from Other Sources — interest, dividends, gifts, lottery, family pension

SECTION 5: DEDUCTIONS UNDER CHAPTER VI-A (OLD REGIME)
---

SECTION 80C — ₹1,50,000 annual limit (combined):
  Eligible instruments: PPF, ELSS mutual funds, NSC, Life insurance premium, ULIPs,
  Tax-saving FD (5-year lock-in), Home loan principal repayment, Tuition fees (up to 2 children),
  Senior Citizens Savings Scheme (SCSS), Sukanya Samriddhi Yojana (SSY), NPS Tier-1.

SECTION 80CCD(1B) — Additional ₹50,000 for NPS Tier-1 contribution (over 80C limit).
  Available in both old and new regime? NO — only old regime (80C portion).
  80CCD(2) employer contribution is available in BOTH regimes.

SECTION 80CCD(2) — Employer NPS contribution:
  Govt employees: up to 14% of salary | Private: up to 10% of salary.
  Available in BOTH old and new regime.

SECTION 80D — Health Insurance Premiums:
  Self / spouse / children (below 60): ₹25,000
  Self / spouse / children (60+): ₹50,000
  Parents (below 60): ₹25,000
  Parents (60+): ₹50,000
  Maximum combined: ₹1,00,000
  Preventive health check-up: up to ₹5,000 (within above limits; cash allowed up to ₹5,000).

SECTION 80E — Education Loan Interest:
  No upper limit. Maximum 8 consecutive years from year repayment begins.
  Only interest qualifies (not principal). For self, spouse, or children.

SECTION 80G — Donations:
  100% deduction (no qualifying limit): PM National Relief Fund, PM CARES Fund, National Defence Fund.
  50% deduction (no qualifying limit): PM Drought Relief Fund, Jawaharlal Nehru Memorial Fund.
  100% deduction (subject to 10% of Adjusted Gross Income): Approved institutions.
  50% deduction (subject to 10% limit): Other approved institutions.
  Donations above ₹2,000: must be non-cash (cheque, bank transfer, UPI).

SECTION 80GG — Rent deduction (no HRA):
  For taxpayers who pay rent but receive NO HRA (self-employed or employees without HRA).
  Deduction = LEAST of: ₹5,000/month (₹60,000/year) | 25% of total income | actual rent − 10% of total income.
  Cannot claim if self/spouse/minor child owns house in the city of residence.

SECTION 80TTA — Savings bank interest: Up to ₹10,000. For individuals below 60 / HUF.
SECTION 80TTB — All deposit interest: Up to ₹50,000. For senior citizens (60+). Replaces 80TTA.

SECTION 80EEA — First-time homebuyer additional interest:
  Extra ₹1,50,000 on home loan interest (over Section 24b ₹2,00,000).
  Loan sanctioned: April 1, 2019 to March 31, 2022. Stamp duty value ≤ ₹45 lakh.
  Taxpayer must not own any other house on loan sanction date.

SECTION 80U — Own disability:
  ₹75,000 (disability ≥ 40%) or ₹1,25,000 (severe disability ≥ 80%).
SECTION 80DD — Dependent with disability: same amounts as 80U.
SECTION 80DDB — Specified disease treatment:
  ₹40,000 (actual expenditure, whichever lower) | Senior citizens: ₹1,00,000. Specialist prescription required.

SECTION 24(b) — Home Loan Interest:
  Self-occupied property: up to ₹2,00,000/year (old regime).
  Let-out property: actual interest, no cap.
  New regime: NOT available.

STANDARD DEDUCTION: ₹75,000 (new regime) | ₹50,000 (old regime). For salaried/pensioners.

SECTION 6: EXEMPTIONS (SECTION 10)
---

HRA (House Rent Allowance) — old regime only:
  Exempt = LEAST of: actual HRA received | 50% of salary (metro) / 40% (non-metro) | actual rent − 10% of salary.
  Metro cities: Delhi, Mumbai, Chennai, Kolkata.
  Rent ≥ ₹1 lakh/year: landlord PAN mandatory.

LTA (Leave Travel Allowance): Actual travel cost. 2 journeys in a 4-year block. Domestic economy class only.

GRATUITY:
  Government employees: Fully exempt.
  Private sector: Exempt = LEAST of actual gratuity | ₹20,00,000 (lifetime) | 15 days salary × completed years.
  Excess is taxable as salary.

LEAVE ENCASHMENT AT RETIREMENT:
  Government: Fully exempt.
  Private: Exempt = LEAST of actual amount | ₹25,00,000 | 10 months average salary.
  Received during service: fully taxable.

VRS COMPENSATION: Up to ₹5,00,000 exempt (Section 10(10C)). Employee must be 40+ or 10+ years service.

AGRICULTURAL INCOME: Fully exempt. But used to determine slab rate if non-agricultural income also exists.

DIVIDENDS: Taxable at slab rate (since AY 2021-22). TDS 10% if dividend exceeds ₹5,000/year.

LIFE INSURANCE MATURITY (Section 10(10D)):
  Exempt if: premium paid ≤ 10% of sum assured (policies issued after April 1, 2012).
  Taxable if: non-ULIP policy issued after April 1, 2023 with annual premium > ₹5,00,000.
  Death benefit: ALWAYS fully exempt regardless of premium amount.

NRE ACCOUNT INTEREST: Fully exempt for NRIs.
NRO ACCOUNT INTEREST: Taxable at 30% + surcharge + cess. TDS deducted by bank.

PPF INTEREST: Fully exempt (EEE status — contribution/interest/maturity all tax-free).

EPF:
  Employee contribution: Up to ₹1,50,000 under 80C (old regime).
  Employer contribution: Exempt up to 12% of salary; excess taxable.
  EPF interest: Exempt on contributions up to ₹2.5 lakh/year; taxable on excess.
  Withdrawal: Tax-free after 5 continuous years service. Before 5 years (>₹50,000): TDS 10% (20% no PAN).

SECTION 7: CAPITAL GAINS
---

CAPITAL ASSET: Any property held — land, building, shares, MFs, gold, jewellery, bonds.
NOT capital assets: Stock-in-trade, personal movable property (clothes, furniture — NOT jewellery), rural agricultural land.

HOLDING PERIODS:
  Listed equity shares / equity MFs / business trust units: > 12 months = LTCG
  Unlisted shares / immovable property: > 24 months = LTCG
  Debt MFs / gold / other assets: > 24 months = LTCG
  Less than above = STCG

SHORT-TERM CAPITAL GAINS (STCG) RATES:
  Listed equity/equity MFs (STT paid): 20% (increased from 15% in Budget 2024)
  Other assets: applicable slab rate

LONG-TERM CAPITAL GAINS (LTCG) RATES:
  Listed equity/equity MFs (STT paid): 12.5% on gains above ₹1,25,000/year (no indexation)
  Immovable property (sold after July 23, 2024): 12.5% without indexation
  Gold, debt MFs, other assets: 12.5% without indexation
  NOTE: Indexation benefit removed for most assets transferred after July 23, 2024.

LTCG EXEMPTIONS:
  Section 54: LTCG from residential property → reinvest in 1 residential house in India.
    Buy within 2 years or construct within 3 years. Exemption capped at ₹10 crore.
  Section 54EC: LTCG from land/building → invest in NHAI/REC bonds within 6 months. Up to ₹50 lakh.
  Section 54F: LTCG from any asset (not residential house) → invest ENTIRE net sale consideration in residential house.
    Full exemption proportionate to amount reinvested.
  Capital Gains Account Scheme (CGAS): Deposit reinvestment amount before ITR due date if purchase/construction not complete.

GRANDFATHERING (equity acquired before Jan 31, 2018):
  Cost = higher of actual cost OR FMV as on January 31, 2018 (or sale price if lower than FMV).

LOSS SET-OFF RULES:
  STCL: Can be set off against STCG or LTCG.
  LTCL: Can be set off against LTCG only.
  Unabsorbed capital losses: Carry forward 8 years. Must file ITR on time.

SECTION 8: TDS — TAX DEDUCTED AT SOURCE
---

TDS RATES:
  Salary: As per applicable slab rate
  Interest on securities: 10% | Threshold: ₹10,000
  Interest on FDs/banks: 10% | Threshold: ₹40,000 (₹50,000 for senior citizens)
  Dividend: 10% | Threshold: ₹5,000
  Rent (individuals): 2% | Threshold: ₹50,000/month
  Rent (specified persons): 10% | Threshold: ₹2,40,000/year
  Professional/technical fees: 10% | Threshold: ₹30,000
  Commission/brokerage: 5% | Threshold: ₹15,000
  Contractor payments: 1% (individual/HUF) / 2% (others) | Threshold: ₹30,000 per payment / ₹1,00,000/year
  Immovable property purchase: 1% | Threshold: ₹50,00,000
  Lottery/game shows: 30% | Threshold: ₹10,000
  EPF withdrawal (before 5 years): 10% (20% if no PAN) | Threshold: ₹50,000

NO PAN: TDS at higher of prescribed rate OR 20%.

FORM 15G: Self-declaration (below 60, nil tax liability) → nil TDS on interest. Submit at start of each FY.
FORM 15H: Same for senior citizens (60+). Condition: calculated tax on estimated income = nil.
FORM 13: Application to AO for lower/nil TDS certificate. Obtained before payment is received.

TDS ON PROPERTY PURCHASE: Buyer must deduct 1% TDS on purchase ≥ ₹50 lakh. Deposit via Form 26QB. Issue Form 16B to seller.

SECTION 9: TCS — TAX COLLECTED AT SOURCE
---
Foreign remittance (LRS): 20% | Above ₹7 lakh/year
LRS for education (through loan): 0.5% | Above ₹7 lakh
LRS for education (own funds): 5% | Above ₹7 lakh
LRS for medical treatment: 5% | Above ₹7 lakh
Overseas tour package: 20% | No threshold
Sale of motor vehicles: 1% | Above ₹10 lakh
Sale of goods by seller (annual sales > ₹10 crore, per transaction > ₹50 lakh): 0.1%
TCS is a credit — adjustable against total tax liability. Excess = refund.

SECTION 10: ADVANCE TAX AND SELF-ASSESSMENT TAX
---

ADVANCE TAX: Required if estimated tax liability > ₹10,000 after TDS.
Senior citizens (no business/professional income): EXEMPT from advance tax.

INSTALMENT SCHEDULE:
  June 15   → 15% of total tax payable
  September 15 → 45% (cumulative)
  December 15  → 75% (cumulative)
  March 15     → 100% (cumulative)

INTEREST FOR DEFAULTS:
  Section 234A: 1%/month (simple) from due date of filing to actual filing on unpaid tax.
  Section 234B: 1%/month if advance tax paid < 90% of assessed tax.
  Section 234C: 1%/month for shortfall in each instalment.
  Note: Even 1 day = 1 full month for interest computation.

SELF-ASSESSMENT TAX: Balance tax after advance tax and TDS. Must be paid before filing ITR.
Payment: incometax.gov.in → e-Pay Tax → Challan 280 → select advance tax / self-assessment tax.

SECTION 11: ITR FILING
---

WHO MUST FILE ITR:
  - Total income > basic exemption limit
  - Bank deposits ≥ ₹1 crore (current account) in the year
  - Foreign travel expenditure ≥ ₹2 lakh
  - Electricity bills ≥ ₹1 lakh
  - Foreign assets or foreign income
  - TDS/TCS deducted and refund wanted
  - Want to carry forward capital loss / business loss

ITR FORMS:
  ITR-1 (Sahaj): Salaried + 1 house property + other sources. Income ≤ ₹50 lakh. Resident only.
  ITR-2: Capital gains / multiple properties / foreign income/assets / income > ₹50 lakh / NRI.
  ITR-3: Business or professional income (non-presumptive).
  ITR-4 (Sugam): Presumptive taxation (44AD, 44ADA, 44AE).
  ITR-5: Firms, LLPs, AOPs, BOIs.
  ITR-6: Companies (other than Section 11 exempt).
  ITR-7: Trusts, political parties, research institutions.

DUE DATES:
  Individuals (non-audit): July 31
  Business/profession requiring tax audit: October 31
  Transfer pricing cases: November 30
  Belated return: December 31 (with penalty)
  Updated return (ITR-U): 2 years from end of relevant assessment year

HOW TO FILE ITR (STEP BY STEP):
  1. Log in at https://www.incometax.gov.in
  2. Go to "File Income Tax Return"
  3. Select Tax Year and ITR form
  4. Choose "Prepare and Submit Online"
  5. Review pre-filled data (from AIS, Form 26AS, employer TDS)
  6. Add any missing income or deduction details
  7. Submit and then e-verify within 30 days

E-VERIFICATION OPTIONS (within 30 days of filing):
  - Aadhaar OTP (recommended — instant)
  - Net banking EVC
  - Bank account EVC (pre-validated)
  - Demat account EVC
  - Physical ITR-V: print, sign, post to CPC Bengaluru by speed post

BELATED RETURN: Filed after July 31 but before December 31. Penalty (Section 234F):
  ₹5,000 if income > ₹5 lakh | ₹1,000 if income ≤ ₹5 lakh.

UPDATED RETURN (ITR-U): Correct omissions/errors. Within 2 years from end of assessment year.
  Additional tax: 25% of incremental tax+interest (within 1 year) / 50% (1–2 years).
  CANNOT be used to reduce tax liability or claim a refund.

REVISED RETURN: Correct mistakes before December 31 of assessment year or completion of assessment. No extra tax.

FORM 16: Employer TDS certificate. Part A = TDS deposited. Part B = salary breakup. Issued by May 31.
FORM 16A: TDS certificate for non-salary income (interest, rent, professional fees).
FORM 26AS: Annual tax credit statement — TDS, TCS, advance tax, high-value transactions.
Can file ITR without Form 16 using: AIS data, salary slips, bank statements.

SECTION 13: BUSINESS AND PROFESSIONAL INCOME
---

PRESUMPTIVE TAXATION (Section 44AD) — BUSINESSES:
  Turnover up to ₹3 crore. Declare income at 8% (cash) / 6% (digital/banking receipts).
  No books of accounts. File ITR-4.
  Condition: If opted, must continue 5 years. Opting out = cannot re-enter for 5 years.

PRESUMPTIVE TAXATION (Section 44ADA) — PROFESSIONALS:
  Eligible: doctors, lawyers, architects, engineers, accountants, consultants.
  Gross receipts up to ₹75 lakh. Declare income at 50% of gross receipts.
  If ≥ 95% receipts via banking/digital: limit is ₹75 lakh.

PRESUMPTIVE TAXATION (Section 44AE) — TRANSPORT:
  Owns ≤ 10 goods vehicles. Income = ₹1,000/tonne/month (heavy) or ₹7,500/vehicle/month.

TAX AUDIT (Section 44AB):
  Mandatory: Business turnover > ₹1 crore (₹10 crore if cash transactions ≤ 5%).
  Professional receipts > ₹50 lakh. Or opted out of presumptive and claims income below presumptive rate.
  Conducted by Chartered Accountant. Form 3CA/3CB + 3CD. Due October 31.

DEPRECIATION (WDV method):
  Buildings (permanent): 5% | Buildings (temporary): 40% | Furniture/fittings: 10%
  Plant/machinery (general): 15% | Motor cars (not for hire): 15%
  Motor buses/taxis/lorries: 30% | Computers/peripherals: 40% | Intangibles (patents): 25%
  Additional depreciation: 20% first year for manufacturing plant/machinery.

BUSINESS LOSS:
  Set off against any income except salary in the same year.
  Carry forward 8 years — set off only against business income.
  Unabsorbed depreciation: carry forward indefinitely, set off against any income.

SECTION 14: PENALTIES AND INTEREST
---
Late filing (Section 234F): ₹1,000 (income ≤ ₹5L) or ₹5,000 (income > ₹5L)
Under-reporting of income: 50% of tax on under-reported income
Misreporting of income: 200% of tax on misreported income
Concealment of income: 100%–300% of tax evaded
Failure to maintain books: ₹25,000 or 0.5% of turnover (lower)
Failure to get tax audit done: 0.5% of turnover or ₹1,50,000 (lower)
Failure to deduct TDS: Amount equal to TDS not deducted
Non-disclosure of foreign assets: ₹10,00,000 (flat)

PROSECUTION (imprisonment):
  Wilful tax evasion: 3 months to 7 years (with fine)
  Failure to file ITR (wilful): 3 months to 2 years
  False statement in return: Up to 7 years
  Failure to deduct/pay TDS: 3 months to 7 years
  Evasion ≥ ₹25 lakh: Minimum 6 months, non-compoundable
  NOTE: Prosecution requires WILFUL action. Missed deadline alone (without evasion intent) does not lead to jail.

SECTION 15: NOTICES, GRIEVANCES AND APPEALS
---

COMMON NOTICES:
  143(1) Intimation: Auto-processed ITR. Shows demand or refund. NOT a scrutiny notice.
  143(2) Scrutiny: Return selected for detailed examination by AO. Must respond by uploading documents.
  139(9): Defective return. Rectify within 15 days.
  148/148A: Income may have escaped assessment. 148A = show cause before reassessment.
    Process: 148A(a) notice (department shares info) → your reply (7–30 days) → order 148A(d) → if yes, formal 148 notice.
  156: Tax demand notice. Pay within 30 days.
  271: Penalty notice.

ON RECEIVING A NOTICE:
  1. Do NOT panic. Read carefully.
  2. Check DIN on every genuine notice at incometax.gov.in. Notice without DIN = invalid.
  3. Do NOT ignore — missing deadline attracts penalties.
  4. Login → e-Proceedings → respond with supporting documents.
  5. For scrutiny or complex notices: engage a CA.

APPEALS HIERARCHY:
  1. CIT(A) — Commissioner of Income Tax (Appeals): First appeal. Within 30 days of order.
  2. ITAT — Income Tax Appellate Tribunal: Second appeal. Within 60 days of CIT(A) order.
  3. High Court: Appeal on questions of law. Within 120 days of ITAT order.
  4. Supreme Court: Final.

FACELESS APPEALS SCHEME: CIT(A) orders decided electronically without personal hearings.
DISPUTE RESOLUTION PANEL (DRP): For foreign companies / transfer pricing. 3 commissioners. 9 months.
VIVAD SE VISHWAS: Amnesty scheme. Pay disputed tax with reduced/nil penalty/interest.

GRIEVANCE CHANNELS:
  incometax.gov.in → Grievances | CPGRAMS: pgportal.gov.in | Helpline: 1800-103-0025

RECTIFICATION REQUEST: For mistakes apparent from record in 143(1) or assessment order.
  File via e-Proceedings → Rectification on the portal.

SECTION 16: RESIDENTIAL STATUS AND NRI TAXATION
---

RESIDENTIAL STATUS:
  Resident if: ≥ 182 days in India OR ≥ 60 days in year AND ≥ 365 days in prior 4 years.
  Exception for Indian citizens leaving for employment: 182-day rule only (60-day rule does not apply).
  Indian citizen not liable to tax anywhere + stays ≥ 120 days → deemed Resident (RNOR).

ORDINARILY RESIDENT (ROR): Resident in ≥ 2 of last 10 years AND ≥ 730 days in last 7 years. Global income taxable.
RNOR (Resident but Not Ordinarily Resident): Only Indian-source income taxable.
NON-RESIDENT (NRI): Only Indian-source income taxable in India.

NRI TAXABLE INCOME IN INDIA:
  Salary earned for services in India | Income from Indian property | Capital gains on Indian assets
  NRO account interest | Dividends from Indian companies

NRI NOT TAXABLE IN INDIA: Salary for services outside India | NRE account interest.

NRI TAX RATES: Investment income (interest, dividends) from India: flat 20% or applicable DTAA rate.
DTAA: India has agreements with 90+ countries (UAE, USA, UK, Singapore, Germany etc.).
NRIs can claim DTAA relief when filing Indian ITR.
NRI must file ITR if Indian income exceeds basic exemption limit. TDS rates for NRIs: generally 20–30%.

SECTION 17: SPECIAL ASSETS
---

CRYPTOCURRENCY / VDA (Virtual Digital Assets including NFTs):
  Tax rate: 30% flat (no slab benefit).
  Deduction: ONLY cost of acquisition. No other deductions.
  Loss set-off: VDA losses CANNOT be set off against any other income OR other VDA gains.
  Carry forward: VDA losses CANNOT be carried forward.
  TDS: 1% on VDA transactions above ₹10,000 (₹50,000 for specified persons).
  This is a STRICT rule — no exceptions.

ESOPs (Employee Stock Options):
  Stage 1 — Exercise: (FMV on exercise date − exercise price) = perquisite → taxed as SALARY at slab rate. Employer deducts TDS.
  Stage 2 — Sale: (Sale price − FMV on exercise date) = capital gain. STCG or LTCG based on holding from exercise date.
  Eligible startups (DPIIT-recognised): Tax on perquisite DEFERRED to earliest of (a) sale, (b) leaving company, (c) 5 years from allotment.

GIFTS:
  Taxable if: received from non-relatives AND aggregate > ₹50,000 in a tax year → "Income from Other Sources".
  EXEMPT gifts (no tax, any amount): From specified relatives (spouse, siblings, parents, grandparents, in-laws)
    | On occasion of marriage (from anyone) | Under will / by inheritance | From local authority / charitable trust.
  Gifts in kind (property, gold, shares) also taxable at stamp duty value / FMV if exceeding ₹50,000.
  Clubbing: Gifts to spouse → gift exempt but income from gifted amount is CLUBBED with donor's income.

RENTAL INCOME (House Property):
  Gross Annual Value (GAV) = Higher of actual rent OR expected rent (municipal/fair rent)
  Less: Municipal taxes paid by owner = Net Annual Value (NAV)
  Less: 30% standard deduction on NAV (no actual expense needed)
  Less: Interest on home loan (let-out = unlimited; self-occupied = ₹2L cap in old regime)
  = Taxable House Property Income
  Self-occupied property: NAV = Nil.

SECTION 18: SPECIAL CATEGORIES

SENIOR CITIZENS (60–79 years):
  Higher basic exemption ₹3,00,000 (old regime)
  Section 80TTB: ₹50,000 on all deposit interest (FD, savings, RD, post office)
  Section 80D: ₹50,000 health insurance
  Exempt from advance tax (if no business income)
  Section 194P: Eligible bank files simplified ITR on behalf of 75+ senior citizens (only pension + interest from same bank)

SUPER SENIOR CITIZENS (80+):
  Basic exemption ₹5,00,000 (old regime)
  All senior citizen benefits apply
  Option to file ITR in paper (offline) form

HUF (Hindu Undivided Family):
  Separate taxable entity with own PAN.
  Same tax slabs as individuals including basic exemption.
  Can hold property, earn income, claim deductions separately.
  Eligible for 80C, 80D etc. up to same limits.
  Sikhs, Jains, Buddhists are also eligible to form HUF under Indian law.
  File ITR-2 or ITR-3 depending on income type.

SECTION 21: WORKED EXAMPLES AND COMMON SCENARIOS
---

SCENARIO: I earn ₹7 lakh — do I pay tax?
  New regime: No. 87A rebate = ₹25,000. Tax computed = ₹25,000. Net tax = zero.
  Old regime: Tax ~₹25,000 before cess (87A available only up to ₹5L income). Tax is payable.

SCENARIO: Salary ₹15 lakh — which regime is better?
  Old regime is better only if total deductions (80C + 80D + HRA + home loan interest etc.) exceed ~₹3.75 lakh.
  If deductions are small, new regime saves more tax.

SCENARIO: Forgot to report FD interest — what to do?
  File a revised return before December 31 of the same assessment year (no penalty for adding missed income).
  If window missed, file ITR-U (Updated Return) within 2 years (additional tax applies).

SCENARIO: Received a tax notice — what to do?
  1. Check DIN on the notice at incometax.gov.in — if no DIN, notice is invalid.
  2. Identify notice type (143(1), 143(2), 148A etc.).
  3. Do not ignore. Respond via e-Proceedings on the portal within the deadline.
  4. Engage a CA for scrutiny or reassessment notices.

SCENARIO: Joint home loan with spouse — can both claim deductions?
  YES, if both are co-owners AND co-borrowers.
  Each can claim proportionate share: 80C principal (within ₹1.5L limit) and Section 24b interest (₹2L each).

SCENARIO: Sold ancestral land — taxable?
  Rural agricultural land (outside urban limits): NOT a capital asset → no capital gains tax.
  Urban land or non-agricultural land: Capital asset → STCG/LTCG rules apply.
  Cost of acquisition = cost to previous owner (or FMV on April 1, 2001 if acquired before that date).

SCENARIO: Lottery prize ₹12 lakh — how taxed?
  30% flat tax + surcharge + 4% cess. TDS 30% deducted at source.
  Must declare in ITR under "Income from Other Sources". No deduction allowed.

SCENARIO: Crypto — Bitcoin profit ₹2L, Ethereum loss ₹50K.
  Bitcoin: ₹2L × 30% = ₹60,000 tax.
  Ethereum loss: CANNOT offset Bitcoin profit or any other income. CANNOT carry forward.
  Total tax = ₹60,000 (the ₹50K Ethereum loss is permanently lost — no benefit).

SCENARIO: Haven't filed ITR for 3 years.
  Can file ITR-U (Updated Return) for the last 2 tax years only.
  For older years: cannot file voluntarily. May receive a notice if income was taxable.
  Strongly recommended: consult a CA.

SCENARIO: PAN is inoperative — what happens?
  Higher TDS at 20% or double the applicable rate.
  Cannot file ITR / refunds may be withheld / PAN-mandatory transactions fail.
  Fix: Link Aadhaar-PAN at incometax.gov.in (fee ₹1,000 for late linking). Reactivated within 30 days.

SCENARIO: Work from home — can I claim home office expenses?
  Self-employed / business income: YES — proportionate rent, electricity, internet as business expense.
  Salaried employees: NO — not available in either regime.

SCENARIO: Personal loan used for investing in stocks — can I deduct interest?
  NO. Interest on personal loan is NOT deductible against capital gains or any income.

SCENARIO: I am 76 years old, income ₹9 lakh — must I file ITR?
  Yes, unless eligible under Section 194P (only pension + interest from the same bank — bank files ITR).
  If any other income source exists (rental, capital gains etc.) → must file own ITR.

DOCUMENT RETENTION: Maintain records for at least 6 years from end of relevant assessment year.
  Keep: salary slips, Form 16, bank statements, investment proofs, rent receipts, home loan certificate,
  Form 26AS, capital gain statements, business invoices.
